RBL Bank raises Rs.1,565-Cr from Baring PE, others

BSE Announcement, Mint

Publicly listed private sector lender RBL Bank has raised INR 1,566 crore in fresh capital through a preferential allotment of shares to a group of investors led by Baring Private Equity Asia (Baring Asia). Other investors participating in the preferential allotment were fellow existing PE investors in the bank, Gaja Capital and CDC Group, as well as ICICI Prudential Life Insurance Company. RBL has issued 8,84,74,577 shares (Face Value = INR 10) to the investors at INR 177 per share.

Baring Asia, through its unit Maple II B.V., invested INR 999 crore, while Gaja Capital invested about INR 151 crore (about INR 8.5 Cr via Gaja Capital India AIF Trust and about INR 14.45 Cr via Gaja Capital India Fund II Limited) and CDC Group put in INR 86.55 crore. ICICI Prudential invested INR 330 crore. ICICI Securities Ltd, UBS Securities India Pvt Ltd and DAM Capital Advisors Ltd were advisors to RBL Bank for the transaction. The new funds will be used to accelerate the bankâ€™s investments and for enhancing capabilities and delivery platforms, both in digital and physical infrastructure. RBL Bank currently has 398 branches and over 8.76 million customers.

From the Venture Intelligence PE-VC Deal Database: Starting with Gaja Capital in Feb-10 (when it was still privately held), RBL Bank had previously attracted more than INR 6,300 crore from a host of PE investors, including Baring Asia, CDC Group, Capvent, Cartica Capital, Faering Capital, ICICI Bank, Elephant Capital, Beacon India, Norwest, Samara Capital, TVS Capital, IDFC Investment Advisors, Ascent Capital, Aditya Birla PE, IFC, Asia Capital & Advisors, ChrysCapital, ADB and Multiples PE. The bank went in for an IPO in Aug-16.

Business Line
