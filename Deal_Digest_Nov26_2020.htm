Laurus Labs buys Richcore Lifesciences for $33-M from Eight Roads, Ventureast

BSE Disclosure

Publicly listed Laurus Labs Ltd has signed definitive agreements with Eight Roads Ventures and VenturEast Proactive Fund and VenturEast Life Fund III, to acquire a 72.55% stake in Bengaluru-based biotechnology company Richcore Lifesciences Pvt Ltd for INR 246.67 crore in cash. The deal will mark Laurusâ€™ entry into the broader biologics and biotechnology segments.

Richcore manufactures animal origin free recombinant products. These products help vaccine, insulin, stem-cell based regenerative medicine and other biopharma customers and eliminate dependency on animal and human blood derived products, which in turn ensures production of safer medicines. For FY20, the company had reported a turnover of INR 41.4 crore.

Edtech co Unacademy raises funding from Tiger Global, Dragoneer at $2-B valuation

Mint

Bengaluru-based educational technology unicorn Unacademy has raised a fresh round of funding from US-based Tiger Global Management and Dragoneer Investment Group, valuing the firm at USD 2 billion.

Fund News

Govt clears equity investment of Rs.6,000-Cr in NIIF debt platform

Mint, Money Control

The Union Cabinet has approved an INR 6,000 crore investment in the equity in National Investment and Infrastructure Fund's (NIIF) debt platform over the next two years. This will help the platform raise INR 1 lakh crore more from private investors by 2025 to finance infrastructure projects. The equity investment for the unit will be sponsored by Aseem Infrastructure Finance Ltd and NIIF Infrastructure Finance Ltd.

The Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (CPPIB) and sovereign wealth fund Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA) have evinced interest in investing in NIIF.

FIR filed against former Xander India executive Ram Reddy for cheating, criminal breach of trust

Economic Times

Based on a complaint filed by Singapore-headquartered The Xander Group, the Chennai police has filed an first information report (FIR) against a former director in its Indian affiliate Ram Reddy and his family members for criminal breach of trust, cheating and forgery. The complaint has highlighted siphoning of funds by Ram Reddy from Gateway Office Parks (GOPL), a company owned by Xander. Ram Reddy had joined the board of Chennai-based GOPL in May 2017 and was also its authorized signatory till October 2020.

GOPL is in the business of construction, development, operation and maintenance of IT and ITES SEZs. It was acquired by Xander in 2017 from Shriram Properties for INR 2,300 crore.
